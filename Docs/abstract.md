# Abstract

**SURYA (Solar Utility for Real-time Yield and Automation)** is a smart renewable-energy prototype designed to improve solar energy utilization through **real-time dual-axis solar tracking** and provide **automatic lighting during low-light or night conditions**. The system uses an **Arduino UNO**, **three LDR sensors**, and **two servo motors** to continuously orient a solar panel toward the direction of maximum sunlight. By comparing the light intensity detected by the sensors, the panel dynamically adjusts in both horizontal and vertical directions for improved energy capture.

In addition to solar tracking, the project also integrates a **solar-powered automatic lighting system**. A **6V solar panel** charges a **3.7V Li-ion battery** through a **TP4056 charging module**, allowing stored solar energy to be used later for lighting. An **LED panel** is controlled through a **MOSFET switch**, and the Arduino determines when to activate the light based on ambient light intensity detected by the LDR sensors. When the environment becomes dark or the light intensity falls below a predefined threshold, the LED automatically turns ON.

The project demonstrates a practical combination of **renewable energy harvesting**, **embedded control systems**, and **sensor-based automation**. It is designed as a **low-cost, educational, and scalable prototype** suitable for applications such as smart solar street lights, garden lighting, and renewable-energy demonstration systems. SURYA highlights how simple embedded electronics can be used to create intelligent and energy-efficient systems for sustainable applications.
